\chapter{Emotional Expressions Similarity}
\label{fig:appendix_emotion_expression_similarity}
\section{Emotion Expression Similarity Task}
Participants were asked to judge how similar the facial expressions of UIBVFED faces were to the facial expressions of RADIATE faces.
These ratings were used to ensure that the emotional expressions of the two datasets were matched in intensity.
A separate sample of 61 participants (41 females and 20 males, M = 20.84, SD = 3.2464, range = 17 to 38) were instructed to compare the expressions themselves rather than the identity or appearance of the faces.
Each trial began with a one-second fixation cross.
After the fixation, a randomly selected pair of photos appeared side-by-side with a slider beneath them for the similarity rating (see Figure \ref{fig:appendix_emotion_expression_similarity_task_diagram}).
The similarity-matching task included three conditions: (1) congruent-congruent (CC): photos matched on both model identity and expression, (2) congruent-incongruent (CI): photos matched on model identity but not on expression, and (3) incongruent-congruent (IC): photos not matched on model identity but matched on expression.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=1\textwidth]{C:/Users/super/OneDrive - Ontario Tech University/fNIRS_Emotions/plots/figures/Expression Rating Task Diagram.png}
    \caption[Emotion Expression Similarity Rating Task]{Diagram of the emotion expression similarity rating task. 
    Participants judged similarity of emotional expression on UIBVFED faces with RADIATE faces.
    Each trial began with a one-second fixation cross, followed by a pair of photos presented side-by-side with a slider beneath them for the similarity rating.}
    \label{fig:appendix_emotion_expression_similarity_task_diagram}
\end{figure}

\section{Results}
To determine whether the faces' physical identity affected similarity judgements, pairwise t-tests were performed contrasting the congruent-congruent (CC) and congruent-incongruent (IC) conditions, with $p$-values adjusted using the Holm procedure.
Only Sadness failed to show a significant difference in similarity ratings across face-feature conditions (\(t(12,999) = -0.051,\; p = 0.95\)), as shown in Figure \ref{fig:appendix_emotion_expression_similarity_results}.

    +3 is the maximum similarity (i.e., identical expressions), and -3 is the minimum similarity (i.e., completely different expressions).}
